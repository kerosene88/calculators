Any decision has possible outcomes. Each has a probability and a payoff. Multiply them together and add — that's Expected Value. Positive EV means the decision is worth taking on average, even if individual outcomes vary. Most people never calculate this. They just go with their gut and wonder why they're stuck.

EV = Σ (probability × payoff)

Bayes' theorem is the mathematically correct way to change your mind. Start with a prior belief, observe evidence, and update proportionally. Not by ignoring evidence — and not by overreacting. The strength of your update should match the strength of the evidence.

P(H|E) = P(E|H) × P(H) / P(E)

Sunk costs are gone regardless of what you decide next. Staying in a bad job because you've already been there 5 years, holding a losing investment because you're down 50%, staying in a relationship because of history — these are all sunk cost traps. The only question that matters: "Would I choose this today if I were starting from scratch?"

Once you've found a genuinely good opportunity, how much should you commit? Too little and you leave money on the table. Too much and variance destroys you before the math pays off. Kelly finds the optimal fraction. Most practitioners use Half or Quarter Kelly for the emotional sustainability. This applies to money, time, career capital — any resource you can allocate more or less of.

f* = (p × b − q) / b  where b = net payoff ratio, p = win prob, q = 1 − p

You only see survivors. The failures are invisible — they don't write books, give interviews, or post on social media. Whenever someone shows you a success story as proof a strategy works, ask: what's the denominator? How many people tried this and failed? If you can't find the denominator, assume the success rate is very low.

True rate = visible successes ÷ (successes + invisible failures)
